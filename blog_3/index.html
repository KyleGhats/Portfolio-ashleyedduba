1.1K SECURITY FORCES TO SECURE THE SPRING FESTIVALS PARADE

As the Spring Festival of 2023 approached, the Baguio City Police Office (BCPO) took proactive measures to ensure the safety and security of all those who would be in attendance. Under the leadership of City Director PCOL Francisco B. Bulwayan Jr., a total of 1,196 security forces were deployed to strategic areas within the city. These forces included 150 volunteers from the Guardians, who were instrumental in helping to monitor and secure the parade venue.nan, was launched on September 19 at Camp Crame in Quezon City.

A briefing for the BCPO personnel was conducted by PLTCOL Domingo M. Gambican, the BCPO Deputy City Director for Operations, to ensure that all preventive measures were in place and that the safety of the public was a top priority. The goal of Baguio's Finest was to have "zero major crime" incidents during the festival, despite the influx of people. The Philippine National Police Academy (PNPA) also played a vital role in the security efforts, with various departments and groups working together to ensure a safe and successful event.

The use of hashtags such as #LifeIsBeautiful, #KaligtasanNyoSagotKo, #TulongTulongTayo, #MKKequalsK, and #PNPKakampiNyo served to remind the public that the BCPO and the PNPA were there to serve and protect, and that the safety and security of all those in attendance at the Spring Festival were of the utmost importance.

Overall, the coordinated efforts of the BCPO, the PNPA, and the various volunteer groups contributed to a safe and successful Spring Festival in 2023. The deployment of 1,196 security forces and the use of various preventive measures helped to ensure the safety and security of all those in attendance, and the goal of "zero major crime" incidents was achieved. This is a testament to the hard work and dedication of all those involved and serves as a shining example of how effective collaboration and teamwork can lead to a successful outcome.
