LEADERSHIP TALKS WITH PLTGEN MALAYO

PLTGEN MALAYO

As a member of the Philippine National Police Academy MASIDTALAK Class of 2023, it is a great honor and privilege to serve the country and the people. As a budding police officer, the first year of service is an opportunity to learn, grow, and hone my wisdom and policy-making potential. It is time to know the gaps and respond to them, even when I am on top. The experience of going through the different rotations and field assignments is invaluable, as it helps me understand the complexities of the job and prepares me for the real challenges ahead.

In dealing with superiors, seniors, colleagues, juniors, and the community, it is important to maintain professionalism and respect at all times. The culture of giving feedback to bosses is crucial to developing a healthy working relationship and creating an environment of open communication. I must be prepared to take the consequences of my actions and not be defensive when receiving feedback from my boss. It is imperative to differentiate work from happy hours and not initiate familiarization with those who are in my chain of command, especially during official times.

It is also important to study the history of the area I am assigned to and the culture of its people. This helps me understand the social dynamics of the community and be aware of the past and current status of their lives. I must respect the customs and traditions that are being observed and be involved in the community I am serving. Preparing my pack lunch and grab bag and anticipating any circumstances is a must, as I must always be ready to be deployed and be well-equipped for any sudden operation. The first impression is lasting, and as a police officer, I must start with my right foot forward. I am responsible for the smallest details, and I am being observed from the first day to my senior days in the service, whether on official or informal functions. My designation will consider the time I have worked from day 1, and I must always strive to improve without wasting time reconstructing. I must avoid haphazard performance of my job and create and establish my trademark in the service by always giving my best. Performing my mandate requires the doctrine of visualization. I must keep myself aware and prepared to do my best, visualize my activities and requirements in the coming days, weeks, months, etc., and figure out how they should be conducted and performed.

Having everything prepared and accomplished before hitting the bunks is essential. No matter if I am invited for dinner or to drink until the early morning by my boss, I must still show that I can still be in my office on time and always be professional. When I am put down by others, I must prove to them that I am worthy of respect without argument. When my boss doesn’t like me, I must keep doing and giving my best, whether he likes me or not, and treat each day as my last day on such an assignment. I cannot choose my boss, but I can establish my credibility by doing my work professionally. When invited to my boss’ quarters or residence, as an officer, I must show up on time and wear smart casual. As the junior officer, I should initiate the conversation in a polite manner.

In dealing with ingratiators, I must not be dictated to by them and not try to win someone’s approval by giving gifts or money. I must simply do my work professionally and not try to win someone’s approval by giving gifts or money. During the director’s time with graduating cadets, it is important to familiarize myself with the context and official time. I must not initiate familiarization during happy hours and always show professionalism.

In conclusion, being a member of the PNPA MASIDTALAK Class of 2023 is a privilege and a huge responsibility. It requires dedication, hard work, and a deep sense of commitment to the ideals of the Philippine National Police Academy. As a member of this prestigious organization, it is imperative that you strive to become a true embodiment of excellence in every aspect of your life. This means not only being technically and tactically proficient but also having a deep sense of social awareness and a commitment to the well-being of the communities you serve.
